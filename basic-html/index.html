Why Accessibility is Important?

Accessibility is the practice of making your websites usable by as many people as possible. We traditionally think of this as being about people with disabilities, but the practice of making sites accessible also benefits other groups such as those using mobile devices, or those with slow network connections.

  • Legal reasons: In some places, it is a legal requirement that websites are accessible to people with disabilities.
  • Moral reasons: We should do our best to ensure our content can be used by as many people as possible.
  • SEO: Search engines give better rankings to sites that are accessible.
  • Mobile: Many of the considerations for accessibility also make your site better for mobile users.

How to Make Accessible Websites?

There are many things you can do to make your websites more accessible, but here are a few things to consider:

  • Providing text alternatives for non-text content.
  • Providing captions and other alternatives for multimedia.
  • Being mindful of the needs of color-blind users.
  • Designing your forms for keyboard access.

Conclusion

Accessibility is important for many reasons, and it is something that we should all be thinking about when building websites. By ensuring your sites are accessible, you are making your content available to a larger number of people, regardless of their abilities.

For more information on accessibility, you can visit the W3C Web Accessibility Initiative.
